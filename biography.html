Chief Justice Earl Warren was raised in California and born on March 19, 1891. He was appointed Cheif Justice by President Dwight Eisenhower in return for the help that Earl Warren had given to President Eisenhower in winning the election. He was appointed for the office on October 2, 1953, and was elected on January 3, 1954. He was involved politically before the oppertunity for Chief Justice presented itself. Some of the roles he played in society included deputy city attorney, deputy assistant district attorney, chief deputy district attorney, district attorney, and attorney genral of California. Afterwards, he became the governor of California for 10 years. Then he was appointed for the Chief Justice and served for 15 years in this prestigious role. Earl Warren retired from this position in 1969, on June 3. He died in Washington D.C. on July 9, 1974. He served in the political process for most of his life as an adult. He was responsible for many changes to the way the country runs. Even though he never became president, despite his attempts to become so as a Republican, he managed to impact history in a great way.
