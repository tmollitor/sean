Chief Justice Earl Warren achieved many things over his long political career. One of his main accomplishments occured at the begining of his career as Chief Justice. He brought down the segregation of schools across the country through his direct influence of the Supreme Court. He created the unanimous decision that occured out of that case, Brown v. Board of Education. He did this because he believed that all people deserved the same education. He also fought for the rights of the accused, and freedom of expression. These goals were achieved through multiple cases. The rights of the accused were increased through the cases Mapp v. Ohio, Gideon v. Wainright, Griffin v. California, Miranda v. Arizona. These cases each did different things to the rights of the accused. He helped freedom of expression the most in the case New York Times Co. v. Sullivan. The intention of the court in this case was to allow the flow of information about government officials, because that is an essential part of the democratic process. Earl Warren achieved multiple goals and helped the way America runs in multiple different ways.
