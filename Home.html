Chief Justice Earl Warren is a man who helped in the court case of Brown v. Board of Education. His parents were immigrants, and he was born on March 19, 1891. He had many jobs related to government prior to his job as a Chief Justice in the judicial branch of government. He was appointed for Cheif Justice by President Eisenhower on October 2, 1953. He was elected by President Eisenhower on January 11, 1954. His leadership in the supreme court created ideas for rights of the accused, freedom of expression, and other revolutionary ideas. He also helped to influence the ruling of the Supreme Court in the case Brown v. Board of Education. The unanimous decision to take down segregation in schools was influenced by Earl Warren. He retired in 1969, on June 3. He died 5 years later, on July 9, 1974, in Washington D.C.
